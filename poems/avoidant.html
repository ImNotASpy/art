Avoidant

Victoria Wallner

October 2023

Avoidance is labeled as wrong,
as hatred, as cold.
But it's fear, really.

Change comes through awareness,
but the anxiety always hits first.

I know I'm not the only one
to push through that panic
just to feel love.

What an ache it is,
to have what you want
right in front of you
but never be able to touch it.

The heart builds an electric fence,
and I flinch at every pulse.

I see closeness in others:
the love, joy, laughter, vulnerability.

And I wonder,
how amazing would that feel,
if only I could let myself?

Instead, the cycle repeats:
get close.
feel safe.
then fear.
then flee.

Too close.
Too much.
Too tight.

The avoidant in me,
it always triggers.

When I try to stay,
I get physically ill:

lump in my throat,
tight chest,
nausea,
shivers for days.

Closeness feels like drowning.
Like I'm being buried alive.

The anxiety screams:
run.
run far away.

Isolation becomes my peace.
Darkness, my comfort.

I vanish, unseen,
safe from the eyes of love.

The avoidant in me asks,
“Why do you want to see me?”

Don't you know?
I am damage.
I am ruthless.
I am stone.

I hurt those who care.
Alone is the only way I know how to survive.

But they say, stronger as a pair.
And I wonder.

Will the damage I cause ever end?

The sadness in their voices,
the loneliness in their eyes,
they confirm what I fear:

I am the damage.

My need to be alone
creates their isolation.

Chaos follows me.

And I can't even look you in the eye,
because it feels like I might die.

Your love makes me sick.
Literally.

So sick I can't move.
So sick I lose my hunger.

They say love cures all,
but for me,
love is a sickness.

Sometimes I wish there was a cure.
An antidote
to take this all away.

But the only thing I can do
is isolate.

Slip into the shadows.
Hide from everyone and everything.

Isolation feels like the only cure
for this disease called love.

Do I want to escape this cage?
I don't know.

The warmth of being alone
feels so natural.

Why does the world revolve around closeness?

Why does distance hurt them,
but heal me?

What they call love,
I call pain.
