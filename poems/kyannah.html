Because of Kyannah

Victoria Wallner

March 2026

Kyannah,
you are teaching me so much.

I did not know I could be soft.
I did not know I could be tender.
I keep finding myself reaching for you
to touch your hair,
your face,
your back.

I lean my head on you.
I sit close to you.

I am not a touchy-feely person.
I never really reach for touch.
I do not really think about it.
Sometimes I have even been a little averse to it.

But you showed me
that this part of me is here.

I did not know it was here.

The way you receive it
and give it back,
and the way I do not retract
or avoid it,
it showed me something.

I did not know
I could be a safe person.

The way you cling to me,
the way you seek comfort with me,
it makes me realize
how much tenderness
and safeness
I was missing in my own past.

I thought I was colder than this.
More detached than this.

I did not know
care could be this simple.

You are teaching my nervous system
that closeness can be uncomplicated.
