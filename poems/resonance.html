Resonance

Victoria Wallner

January 2026

Some people feel at ease
without knowing why.

Others pause at the edge,
sensing depth beneath the surface,
unsure whether to step in.

Depth asks for discomfort.
It asks for stillness long enough
to notice what stirs beneath the calm.

Presence with the self
makes space for another.

Some step forward
and stay with it
long enough
to find their balance.

Some leave not only because the water is cold,
but because the distance is forgiving.

What is missed is not intensity,
but resonance.

The quiet understanding
that forms when two depths meet,
and neither needs to flee.

Two people may stand close,
yet never meet.

What's missing is not closeness,
it's the moment
where something real
could have stayed.
