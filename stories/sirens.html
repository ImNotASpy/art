The Loud Sound of Sirens

Victoria Wallner

November 2020

Kasim turns a corner after an unsightly double-take. Did I really see that? Kasim continues his fast pace down the almost vacant sidewalk. He remembered the faceless being that he laid eyes on moments ago. It had the facial structure, but no features. How could this be possible? And it was just standing there, not even facing in my direction.

A lady interrupts Kasim's thoughts.

"Hey, what the hell, watch where you are going!"

"Oh, sorry," Kasim mutters and barely glances at the angered woman.

He continues his walk back to his car, which was six blocks away. Kasim rubs his aching forearm from where he had just bumped into a table that the bartender was cleaning. Out of the corner of his eye, he saw it. The faceless being was standing next to a parked car on the street.

This time he got to process the appearance. Kasim turned his head in the direction of the being, but it was not there.

"Maybe you could only see it out of your peripherals?" he thought.

The eyes were sunken in, and there was no hole where the mouth should have been. It was a light pink color and naked. The naked body was that of a thin old man, like it had not eaten for years.

Kasim looked behind him and tried to distract himself with the vibrant colors of the restaurant's exterior that he passed.

"Don't think about it, Kaz," Kasim whispers to himself.

I knew I stayed up way too late helping Josh with his music. I need to learn how to take a break once in a while.

The loud sound of sirens wailed beside him. Kasim looked in the direction of the ambulance and saw the faceless being on the edge of the sidewalk, facing him.

Startled, Kasim broke into a sprint. Only three more blocks to go.

What the hell was that? What does it want from me?

Kasim listened to the scuff of his feet across the sidewalk with each step. Didn't anyone else see this thing? He looked around and realized that he was almost alone.

Kasim stopped running. Two more blocks.

Taking deep breaths, Kasim walked down the linear path, praying that he would make it to his car on time. He could see the faceless being, from his peripherals, sitting on the nearby steps of a large red apartment. It was facing him as he walked to his destination.

Kasim knew better than to look at it. It seemed like every time he made eye contact, the creature appeared closer.

Why did I park so far away from our meeting spot!? I should have known it would have taken until 2 a.m. to finish Josh's music.

Kasim reached into his pocket and felt around for his keys.

Wait a minute. What is this?

Kasim stopped moving. He pulled out a long, thick strand of black hair.

What the fuck.

He let the hair fall to the ground. Kasim heard a noise and looked ahead of him.

There it was. The faceless being.

It stood in front of him. It was taller than Kasim. And oh, that sound it was making. The sound of gurgling liquids was pulsing from the creature.

Kasim froze in fear. He closed his eyes tight, thinking that this could be a dream. When he opened them, the creature was gone.

Fuck, man. Did I do shrooms without knowing again?

Kasim shook his head and jogged the last block back to his car. He could see the shiny blue BMW glisten in the street lamp. He reached the side of his car and fumbled for his keys.

Ah, left pocket.

Kasim unlocked his car using the key remote and went to open his door. What he saw in the glass window was the most horrifying thing he had ever seen. Kasim did not see his own reflection, but in its place was the faceless being.
